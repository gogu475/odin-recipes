Stewed tripe and onions

Tripe is the first or second stomach of a cow or other ruminant used as food. Its use as a term of execration is a load of tripe.

Ingredients

  • 2lb tripe
  • 5 large onions
  • 3 cloves
  • 2 oz butter
  • 2 oz flour
  • 1.5 pt milk
  • pepper and salt
  • 1 tbsp white breadcrumbs
  • 0.25 pt cream

Method

  1. Wash the tripe in several lots of cold water. Cut into pieces 1 inch square.
  2. Put the tripe, sliced onions, cloves, butter, and milk in a saucepan. Cover with a tight fitting lid. Bring to the boil and allow to simmer gently until the tripe is tender-about 75 mins.
  3. Lift out the cloves. Thicken with blended flour and allow to cook for a further 5 minutes. Season and add cream. Finally add in breadcrumbs. Serve on a hot dish with tripe in the centre and sauce pured over and around.
